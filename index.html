Starting Out

Twenty One Pilots (formed in 2011 in Columbus Ohio) is an alternative hip hop band (fans labeled as Schizoid pop) consisting of Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun, the former drummer of House of Heroes. Their purpose for making music is "to make people think", as well as encourage them to find joy in what they come to believe in life. Tyler Joseph plays piano, keyboards, synthesizers, ukulele, bass, drums, percussion as well as being the lead vocalist. Josh plays drums, percussion, and is the backing vocals as of 2011. Nick Thomas and Chris Salih were in the band at the beginning but left in 2011 due to busy schedules.

Music

They have three studio albums, Twenty One Pilots (2009), Vessel (2013), and Blurryface (2015) with the latest two having the label Fueled by Ramen. "Heathens" used for the movie Suicide Squad (2016), written by Tyler Joseph and produced by him along with Mike Elizondo. The song was leaked on he internet on June 15, 2016, the same day Twenty One Pilots tweeted a message in morse code saying "takeitslow" which was a lyric to the song. They've been on 4 concert tours as well as joining Fall Out Boy and Panic! at the Disco in the Save Rock and Roll Tour in 2013. The band also won 7 awards, as well as being nominated for 15 awards.
